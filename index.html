Down Garrapata Road | Exploring Mexican-American Culture


Down Garrapata Road is a heartwarming chronicle of the lives of a series of families living in South Texas during World War II. As it starts by peering into the life of one family on the farm road, and moves down the line, it paints a vivid picture of what life was like for these young Mexican-Americans in a world on the brink of change. Told entirely in the voices of children, Anne Estevis brings a fresh and unique perspective to the World War II era, and introduces the readers to the aspects of Mexican-American culture. The stories of Chatita, Paco, Nilda, Zulema, and Teo portray a world that is new and foreign, yet strangely and welcomingly familiar. As children living in a generation of both cultural and political challenges, they must each make sacrifices and mistakes as they grow and learn in all ways.
